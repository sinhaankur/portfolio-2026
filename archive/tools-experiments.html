GovLens: Accessibility + Trust for Government Websites

Many users are targeted by fake websites that mimic official portals. GovLens is designed to place clear trust information inside the browser so users can quickly decide whether a site is likely official, readable, and safer to use.

1) Government Domain Verification

GovLens checks domain patterns commonly used for official Government of India properties.

  • Highlights expected official patterns such as .gov.in and .nic.in.
  • Flags suspicious lookalike domains for caution.
  • Shows quick visual trust status before users share sensitive details.

2) Accessibility-First Trust Layer

Accessible websites are typically better maintained and easier to trust and navigate, especially for first-time users.

  • Checks key readability and structure signals in-page.
  • Surfaces practical alerts users can understand without technical jargon.
  • Supports safer browsing for citizens with varied digital literacy.

3) Scam Risk Reduction in Real Time

Instead of expecting users to investigate manually, GovLens brings risk context directly where browsing happens.

  • Warns users when trust signals are weak or inconsistent.
  • Encourages verification before payments, OTP entry, or document upload.
  • Builds confidence for everyday public-service interactions online.

4) Public Trust by Design

GovLens combines usability, accessibility, and security awareness into one extension workflow.

  • Simple status messaging for quick decision-making.
  • Designed as a citizen-first utility rather than a technical dashboard.
  • Helps keep trust anchored in the browser, where scams begin.

Built & Designed by Ankur Sinha

GovLens is live on the Chrome Web Store. The goal is straightforward: reduce uncertainty, improve accessibility confidence, and help users avoid fraudulent lookalike websites.

How AI Design Is Used Now

AI in design has moved from novelty to everyday production workflow. Strong teams are not using AI to replace product thinking, but to compress repetitive loops so more time goes into systems thinking, user clarity, and strategic decisions.

1) Discovery and Opportunity Mapping

AI accelerates synthesis in the early phase by clustering qualitative notes and surfacing recurring user intent patterns.

  • Interview transcript summarization with theme extraction.
  • Auto-tagging support tickets into friction categories.
  • Opportunity maps ranked by user pain and business impact.

2) Information Architecture and Content

Designers are using AI to draft first-pass structures while retaining editorial ownership for tone and domain precision.

  • Alternative navigation trees from the same content corpus.
  • Microcopy variants for errors, confirmations, and onboarding.
  • Terminology simplification for plain-language UX.

3) Interaction and Prototyping

AI helps teams expand exploration breadth by generating multiple flow options and edge-case states before committing to high-fidelity build.

  • Flow alternatives for novice vs expert pathways.
  • State coverage drafts for empty, error, and loading scenarios.
  • Prompt-to-prototype snippets for rapid interaction testing.

4) Design Systems and QA

In mature teams, AI is integrated into design system governance to improve consistency and reduce UI debt.

  • Token usage checks across screens.
  • Component mismatch detection against system rules.
  • Accessibility pre-checks for contrast and labeling gaps.

Practical AI Design Operating Model

Best results come from a human-led loop: Frame the problem, Generate variants, Evaluate with UX criteria, Validate with users, and Institutionalize what works into system standards. AI is strongest in generation and compression; humans remain accountable for judgment and outcomes.

How AI Research Is Evolving

AI research in product teams now combines qualitative rigor with scalable signal processing. The goal is not to automate insight, but to increase confidence by triangulating multiple data layers faster.

1) Multi-Source Insight Pipelines

Research is increasingly continuous and instrumented rather than periodic and presentation-driven.

  • Combining interviews, session replay, analytics, and support logs.
  • Semantic clustering to detect emerging behavior shifts.
  • Weekly evidence snapshots instead of quarterly report decks.

2) Agent-Assisted Desk Research

Researchers use AI agents for rapid environmental scans, then manually validate source quality and interpretation.

  • Competitive teardown drafts with evidence references.
  • Policy and standards extraction for compliance-heavy domains.
  • Pattern-library retrieval from prior internal studies.

3) Evidence Quality and Bias Controls

The strongest AI-research practices include explicit guardrails to prevent false confidence.

  • Traceability: every claim mapped to a source artifact.
  • Bias checks: demographic and behavioral representativeness review.
  • Confidence labels: high, medium, or exploratory findings.

4) Metrics That Matter

Teams are shifting from output metrics to decision metrics that show real product movement.

  • Time-to-insight from raw data to actionable recommendation.
  • Decision adoption rate in roadmap and release planning.
  • Outcome lift after changes: completion, error reduction, retention.

Applied AI Research Loop

A reliable loop is: Question (define decision risk), Collect (mixed methods), Synthesize (AI + human review), Test (prototype and experiment), Close the loop (measure post-launch behavior). This turns research into an operating system for product quality, not a one-time artifact.
