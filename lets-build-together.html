How I work at Principal level

Three things that change at this altitude

Senior designers ship a feature well. Principals make sure the right feature ships, in a way the rest of the org can build on. Here's what that looks like in practice on the work I do today.

01 · Strategy

Framing the problem before the screen

Most "design problems" are misframed product problems. I spend the first week of any engagement with PM and engineering leadership re-asking what we're actually solving for, who pays the cost of getting it wrong, and what good looks like in numbers.

  • Outcome framing with PM/Eng leadership
  • Competitive teardowns & opportunity sizing
  • UX north-star + measurable success criteria
02 · Systems

Designing patterns, not screens

At Principal scope, every flow is a chance to extend the system. I ship the screen and the reusable pattern — and treat adoption by other teams as the success metric, not approval from a single review.

  • Design-system contribution (HgDS / RDS)
  • IA & interaction patterns for dense, operator-grade surfaces
  • Cross-service consistency reviews (UX Review Board)
03 · People

Multiplying the team's output

The most leveraged thing a Principal does isn't pixels — it's raising the bar for everyone around them. I mentor designers across teams, partner on hiring, and codify the working practices that let the next ten flows ship without my hands on them.

  • Cross-team design mentorship
  • Hiring loops & rubric calibration
  • Critique cadence, review process, design ops
